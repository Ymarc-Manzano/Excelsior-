Gonzales Sails Through R1AA, Clinches Palaro Stint

By Ymarc Immanuel Manzano

Sean Nathan Gonzales of Christ the King College once again bucketed several medals in the swimming competition of the Region 1 Athletic Association (R1AA) held in Alaminos, Pangasinan, Feb. 11-15.

The Palarong Pambansa-veteran Gonzales took home silver for 200m Individual Medley, 100m Butterfly, and 200m Butterfly while also taking bronze for the 100m Breaststroke and 200m Breaststroke.

By garnering the following accolades, Gonzales has now qualified to compete in the Palarong Pambansa 2018 in the categories of 200m Individual Medley, 100m Butterfly, and 200m Butterfly.

"My training now is not as easy as it was before. Now I need to train harder and be more disciplined," stated Gonzales of 10-2.

His sister Beyonce Gonzales, three-time Palaro bronze medalist, also bagged silver medals in 4×50 Freestyle Relay and 4x100 Freestyle Relay.

"I’m happy with the results of the competition and I thank God for guiding and blessing me and my sister with such a talent," expressed Gonzales.

The duo was trained by their three-year coach Bayani Gavino at Panicsican, San Juan, La Union.

CKC Ousts UCC, 75-72

By Ymarc Immanuel Manzano

Reviving their momentum in the second half, Christ the King College (CKC) snatched a come-from-behind victory against the powerhouse Union Christian College (UCC), 75-72, in their clash for the Student Body Organization (SBO) Basketball League Games at the Catbangen Covered Court, 7:30 pm, February 5.

On the 1st half, the UCC basketball team checked the defense of CKC and continued in scoring the basket with a tight defense and aggressive gameplay.

Luigi Bañezta, team captain of UCC, unleashed his dribble-drive move and three point shooting skills to cement the lead and control the early quarters of the game, ending the 1st half 45-33 in their favor.

Entering the second half, CKC improved their offense-defense play and chased the lead of UCC with back-to-back shots on rainbow territory.

The dynamic duo of 5’10” Maynes, power forward of CKC, and John Paolo Paraguas brought back their rhythm by continuously scoring along the paint to tie up the game to 70-70

“We had the dedication to win the game and we wanted to represent our school in the best way we can,” expressed Carl Lubrica, from CKC.

Continuing their impressive man-to-man defense, CKC eventually cemented their triumph over UCC as the buzzer sounded, 75-72.

“Team work and team play,” highlighted Romar Pimentel. “We worked together as a team so that we could achieve the victory even without a coach. We just wanted to represent the school very well and I'm glad we did,” he continued.

After their win against UCC, the team will face off against one of the dominating teams in the SBO Basketball League, the Saint Louis College (SLC) team led by Krishan Agulan.

Sports Editorial: In Support of Those Who Raise the Flag

By Ymarc Immanuel Manzano

Taking part in the Olympics is the dream for most athletes - a prestigious international sporting event where competitions are held from a myriad of sports. Luckily, this dream has become a reality for Filipino athlete Michael Martinez, a figure skater who competed in the 2018 PyeongChang Winter Olympics. But behind the blaze and glory of taking part in the Olympics and representing his homeland, lack of support manifested in his journey, and it took a toll in our country’s performance.

First of all, seldom does a Filipino athlete ever get the chance to compete in the prestigious Winter Olympics with Martinez and alpine skier Asa Miller being the only ones this year. With this given, in true Filipino fashion, moral support should have been evident for the country’s representatives which is something that Filipinos have always took pride of when it comes to international pageants and bouts of ‘People’s Champ’ Manny Pacquiao. Unfortunately in this instance, it seemed to be the total opposite and it showed.

In connection, there was also lack of exposure in the media. Majority did not even know that the Philippines was joining the said Olympic event. Unlike the attention given to other international competitions with pageants being a notable example, not even half of that was visible in support of Martinez who ended up finishing 28th in the Short Program for Men’s Figure Skating, failing to advance in the other round of the competition.

If not for Sweden’s entry withdrawing from the competition, Martinez would have already been out for the winter games following his eighth-place finish in the Nebelhorn Trophy in Germany last year, an event that also garnered meager attention. What we can take from this is that the current state of sports in the Philippines is far from global standards. Insufficient facilities and financial assistance have long been the woes of athletes and they all point to one root cause, dirty politics in sports.

This is made all the worse with the Philippine Sports Commission stating that sports is not a priority for the government, and athletes are the ones who suffer from this the most.

The Olympics, being an internationally-renowned event, in which we are able to send our athletes, is something that our country should acknowledge more; the same also goes for the athletes themselves. Supporting those who banner our flag in a global scale with the likes of Martinez and Miller would actually help in boosting their performance. Representing our country is not and will never be an easy feat. After all, their performance reflects the country of our own, so they need all the support they can get.
